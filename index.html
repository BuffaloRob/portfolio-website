Bio

I joined the United States Navy straight out of high school and spent my first year and a half going through the nuclear power training pipeline. I went on my first deployment six months after arriving onboard the USS Enterprise in Norfolk, VA. By the time my second deployment came around, I was fully qualified and leading a team of people operating one of the six machinery rooms onboard. I spent my last two years in the Navy working exclusively in quality assurance. After leaving the Navy, I figured I'd exercise some of my benefits and decided to try college. I first pursued an engineering degree, it closely related to what I did while in the Navy and made sense at the time. Quickly, I became attracted to entrepreneurship and business in general. I worked towards my business degree for 2 years until I realized I wasn't really learning much that I couldn't pick up from a few good books. A serendipitous call with a friend helped my decision to change my path away from college and move to Chicago. There, I learned how to become a critical facilities technician working in data centers. After two years I was offered my own data center to manage in Charlotte, NC. Not only was this an advancement in my career, but it allowed me to the opportunity to live much closer to my family. Once I had my data center running smoothly, I enrolled in Flatiron's online software engineer program and worked on it in my spare time. I was able to complete the course in June 2019.
Somewhere along the way, I developed an urge to go backpacking and take pictures of the places I went to. I also enjoy working on my car and participating in autocross and high-performance driving events.
