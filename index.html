Bio

I joined the Navy straight out of highschool and spent my first year and half going through the nuclear power training pipeline. I went on my first deployment six months after arriving onboard the USS Enterprise. By the time my second deployment came around I was fully qualified and leading a team of people operating one of the six machinery rooms onboard. I spent my last two years in the Navy working exclusively in quality assurance. After leaving the Navy I figured I'd exercise some of my benifits and decided to try college. I first persued an engineering degree because it closely related to what I did while in the Navy but quickly became attracted to entrepenueship and business in general. I stayed with the business degree for 2 years until I realized I wasn't really learning much that I couldn't pick up from a few good books. A serendipitous call with a friend made me decide to drop out of college and move to Chicago to learn how to become a critical facilites technician working in data centers. After two years I was offered my own data center to manage and took the opportunity to move to Charlotte which also allowed me to be closer to my family. Once I had my data center running smoothly I enrolled in Flatiron's online software engineer program and worked on it in my spare time and I was able to complete the course in June 2019. Somewhere along the way I developed an urge to go backpacking and take pictures of the places I went to. I also enjoy working on my car and participating in autocross and high performance driving events.
